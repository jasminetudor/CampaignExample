What's Going On

Alabama has seen a dramatic drop in staff and increase in poor management throughout it’s prison system. Nowhere is this more evident than within the St. Clair Correctional Facility where Rogarius Bray was fatally stabbed on September 20, 2018—the second homicide at the facility in less than three weeks.


Delays in implementing remedial measures and the failure to institute corrective action has led to a crisis within Alabama prisons like St. Clair. Stabbings, sexual assaults and homicides have become regular occurrences. This is a human right’s crisis occurring within Alabama jurisdiction.

Within St. Clair

there is often no officer present in housing units. Rogarius Bray was killed in what they call a “hot bay” unit, or a unit where men with higher management needs are assigned. As a result to the staffing crisis, officers rarely go into this dorm despite the assignment of higher management need-inmates. No programming or management is provided. Similar situations are occurring in other “hot bay” units within facilities like Bibb Correctional Facility and Ventress Correctional Facility—where four homicides in the past year have occurred.

With homicides, sexual assaults, stabbings and robberies becoming a daily occurrence help us call upon Governor Kay Ellen Ivey to take back control of prisons, and protect prisoners from the violence occurring behind bars.
